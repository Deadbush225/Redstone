Viscosity is a measure of a fluid's resistance to flow, describing the internal friction within a moving fluid. It characterizes how easily a fluid can flow, with high viscosity indicating strong resistance to motion due to internal friction, while low viscosity signifies easy flow with minimal internal friction. Factors Affecting the Capillary Action:

This phenomenon is evident in everyday life, where honey flows more readily when warmed compared to its sluggish movement at room temperature which arises due to the interplay of different forces:

1. Temperature

Viscosity is inversely proportional to temperature for liquids, meaning as temperature increases, viscosity decreases.

In gases, viscosity is directly proportional to temperature.

2. Fluid Composition

The molecular makeup of a fluid influences its viscosity, with different substances exhibiting varying levels of resistance to flow.

3. Pressure

Changes in pressure can impact a fluid's viscosity, especially in complex fluids like pastes or gels where pressure affects their flow behavior.

Viscosity can be influenced by shear rate, where the speed of fluid movement relative to the distance it moves affects its resistance to flow.

WHO???

Jean-Louis-Marie Poiseuille

Driven by a fascination with blood circulation in the human body, French physician and physiologist Jean-Louis-Marie Poiseuille embarked on a series of experiments that not only revolutionized our understanding of blood flow but also led to the groundbreaking discovery of viscosity.

This fundamental property, representing a fluid's resistance to flow, transcended the boundaries of physics to profoundly impact medical science.

Poiseuille's work laid the foundation for future explorations in fluid dynamics, making his contribution a cornerstone in our comprehension of the behavior of both liquids and gases across various scientific disciplines.
