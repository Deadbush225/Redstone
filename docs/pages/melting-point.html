The melting point is the temperature at which a solid substance transitions into a liquid state. It is a crucial physical property used to identify substances, with pure compounds melting over a narrow range compared to impure ones. Let‚Äôs dive into the details:

Factors Affecting the Melting Point:

Melting points are determined by heating a sample and observing the temperature range over which the solid transforms into a liquid, which arises due to the interplay of different forces:

1. Purity

Impurities lower and widen the melting point range of substances, affecting their overall melting behavior.

2. Intermolecular Forces

Stronger intermolecular forces lead to higher melting points as more energy is required to break these bonds.

3. Molecular Weight

Compounds with higher molecular weights tend to have higher melting points due to increased van der Waals forces between molecules.

When these forces combine, they pull the liquid into the tube or material, allowing it to rise against gravity.

4. Pressure

Changes in pressure can alter the melting point of substances, especially for materials sensitive to pressure variations.

The melting point range is indicative of purity, where impurities cause a lower and wider melting range.
