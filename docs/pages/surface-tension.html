Surface tension is a physical characteristic representing the force required to expand the surface of a liquid per unit area. Surface tension arises from cohesive forces between liquid molecules, causing a thin, elastic layer on the surface. This phenomenon, driven by molecules at the surface experiencing a net inward force, affects various behaviors like capillary action and droplet shape.

Factors affecting Surface Tension:

Surface tension, also known as surface energy, is influenced by factors such as:

1. Surfarcants

The term "surfactant" refers to surface-active agents. They are amphiphilic compounds, with hydrophilic and hydrophobic chemical structures.

2. Impurities

Impurities, whether on the surface or dissolved in a substance, directly impact the liquid's surface tension.

Adding highly soluble impurities to water, for instance, leads to an increase in its surface tension. The presence of impurities in a liquid has a notable effect on surface tension.

3. Temperature

The temperature is an important factor in affecting the surface tension of a liquid. In general, surface tension decreases as temperature rises and increases as temperature falls.

Temperature affects it, decreasing with higher temperature and increasing with lower temperature. Dissolved substances and oxidation increase it. Oxygen lowers surface tension. Impurities, soluble or not, impact surface tension. Contaminants like oil reduce surface tension.

Milk fireworks experiments involve the use of milk and food coloring to construct a small dome. 2. Soap Boat

Soap Boat is an experiment that involves the use soap to change the surface tension of the water to propel their boats. The use of soap/detergent weakens the attraction of water molecules to one another (cohesion). Surface tension is caused by the attraction of water particles at the surface of the water.

3. Floating Card

The index card is held in place by atmospheric pressure (the pressure applied by the surrounding air). The card remains atop the upside-down cup or glass because the pressure of the air molecules pressing up on it exceeds the weight of the water pushing down. Observations assist in revealing the unusual water suspension. So the physics behind this floating water trick is just the surface tension across the screen, which holds the water in place.
