Surface tension is a physical characteristic representing the force required to expand the surface of a liquid per unit area. Surface tension arises from cohesive forces between liquid molecules, causing a thin, elastic layer on the surface. This phenomenon, driven by molecules at the surface experiencing a net inward force, affects various behaviors like capillary action and droplet shape.

Definition:

Capillary action, also known as capillarity, capillary motion, or wicking, arises due to the interplay of two types of forces:

  • Cohesive forces: These forces hold the liquid molecules together.
  • Adhesive forces: These forces exist between the liquid and the material of the tube or porous substance.

When these forces combine, they pull the liquid into the tube or material, allowing it to rise against gravity.
